## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Whitepapper is a content platform where you write once and do everything — publish on your site, distribute to other platforms, serve it to your app through an API, and let AI agents read and write it.

**What it does:** Whitepapper gives you a place to create and organize content, then get it anywhere. You get SEO-optimized public pages out of the box, a developer API to pull content into any app or site, platform integrations to push content to Dev.to, Hashnode, Medium, and others, and an MCP server so AI agents like Claude, Copilot, and Cursor can read and write your content directly from an IDE. Pages are edge-cached so they're fast globally without any extra setup.

**Product type:** API-first content platform (web app + developer API).

**Business model:** Currently free to use. No paid pricing is live yet.

---

## Who It's For

Whitepapper is built for anyone who creates content and wants it to actually work for them — not sit in one tool and go nowhere.

**Primary audiences:**

- **Indie developers and builders** who want their portfolio, blog, docs, and project updates in one place without maintaining a separate CMS or static site setup.
- **Small businesses and startups** that need a simple, fast content backend without the overhead of enterprise CMS tools.
- **Technical writers and content creators** who want to publish across multiple channels from one source without copy-pasting.
- **Teams building content-driven products** who want structured content served through an API rather than locked inside a UI.

**Who it's not for:** Large editorial teams needing complex approval workflows, enterprises needing deep custom infrastructure, or non-technical users who want a drag-and-drop website builder.

---

## Problems It Solves

**The core problem:** Content ends up fragmented. You write something, then manually copy it to your site, then copy it again to three publishing platforms, and six months later you can't remember which version is the canonical one. Your app pulls content from a Google Doc. Your blog is on Notion. Your docs are somewhere else entirely.

**What this costs:** Time, duplicated effort, inconsistent SEO metadata, no single source of truth, and a constant feeling that publishing should not require this much operational work.

**Why current tools fall short:**

| Tool | Where it falls short |
|------|----------------------|
| Traditional CMS (WordPress, Ghost) | Heavy to set up, not API-first, overkill for most content workflows |
| Notion / local markdown | No built-in distribution, no real API, terrible SEO defaults |
| Publishing directly on Dev.to or Hashnode | Platform owns your content, no canonical control, no API reuse |
| Headless CMS tools | Developer-friendly but often expensive, over-engineered for solo or small-team use |

---

## What Makes It Different

Whitepapper treats content as structured data that belongs to you — not a blob of text locked inside a UI.

**Key differentiators:**

- **SEO built in.** Meta tags, Open Graph, canonical URLs, structured data, and sitemap generation are automatic. You don't configure anything.
- **A real content API.** Every project and collection gets a read API so the same content can power a personal site, a product docs page, a portfolio, or a custom app without any extra plumbing.
- **MCP for AI agents.** AI tools like Claude, GitHub Copilot, and Cursor can read and write your content directly through Whitepapper's MCP server, without leaving the IDE.
- **Platform distribution built in.** Push content outward to Dev.to, Hashnode, Medium, and more without leaving Whitepapper.
- **Fast by default.** Public pages are edge-cached globally. No performance tuning needed.
- **No lock-in.** You own the canonical. Whitepapper pushes content to other platforms, not the other way around.

## Competitive Positioning

**Direct:** Ghost, Contentful, Sanity, and similar CMS or headless CMS tools. They are either too heavy, too expensive, or not built around a publish-and-distribute workflow.

**Indirect:** Publishing directly on Dev.to, Hashnode, or Medium with no central source. Or duct-taping together Notion + a custom site + manual cross-posting.

**Our position:** Whitepapper is the content backend for people who want to move fast, own their content, and make it usable in multiple places without standing up a full CMS stack.

## Brand Voice

**Style:** Clear and simple. Technical when useful. Personal when appropriate.

**Words to use:** content platform, content API, publish once, distribute everywhere, edge-cached, MCP, canonical control, structured content, developer-friendly.
